About the Specialty

Mechanical engineering centered on systems, structures, and performance

This specialty combines mechanical design, structural reasoning, material selection, simulation, industrial drawing, and production methods. It prepares students to develop complete mechanical solutions from first concepts to technical validation.

Design & Construction

Build robust parts and assemblies with attention to geometry, kinematics, tolerance chains, serviceability, and drafting quality.

Simulation & Analysis

Evaluate stresses, deformation, thermal behavior, fluid effects, and dynamic response to support sound engineering decisions before fabrication.

Manufacturing Integration

Connect design intent with machining, forming, welding, and assembly constraints so that concepts remain practical, efficient, and production-aware.

Engineering Workflow

  • Translate requirements into clear mechanical concepts and functional layouts.
  • Model parts and assemblies with design intent, fit, and manufacturability in mind.
  • Validate performance using structural, thermal, and motion-focused analysis.
  • Prepare drawings, reports, and recommendations for academic or industrial review.

Workshop & Production Awareness

  • Machining process awareness for milling, turning, setup, and fixture logic.
  • Foundational knowledge of welding, forming, assembly, and shop-floor constraints.
  • Attention to inspection, tolerances, surface finish, and repeatability.
  • Practical mindset for balancing performance, cost, and production feasibility.
